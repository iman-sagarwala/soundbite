the problem

reading shouldn't feel like
a wall you can't climb.

Millions of people — students with dyslexia, people with ADHD, visual impairments, or just anyone who processes information better through sound — are handed text-heavy documents and told to figure it out. Existing text-to-speech tools help, but they all sound the same: robotic, cold, impersonal.

Engagement matters. A student is far more likely to absorb a history lecture if it sounds like Heimler than if it sounds like a synthesizer. A child with reading difficulty is more likely to follow along if the voice is familiar, warm, and human.

01
voice that feels human
Clone any real voice from recordings. Not a preset — the actual cadence, rhythm, and character of a person you trust.
02
personality, not just sound
We transcribe recordings and model speaking style, so the voice doesn't just sound like them — it reads like them too.
03
any document, anywhere
PDFs, images, web pages — upload or hover to select text. Your voice reads it aloud in seconds through the browser extension.
